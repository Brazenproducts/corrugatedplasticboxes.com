The Cold Chain Packaging Problem

Cold chain logistics destroys packaging from two directions simultaneously: freezing makes cardboard brittle and causes it to crack under load; thawing allows moisture absorption that collapses structural integrity. A box that survives the outbound frozen shipment often fails on the return leg or at the distribution center when temperatures rise.

PP corrugated boxes are chemically unaffected by temperature across the entire cold chain range. They don't absorb moisture, don't become brittle at sub-zero temperatures, and maintain their compression strength from blast freezer to ambient dock. The same box that stacks safely at -20°C in frozen storage stacks safely at 25°C in a distribution center — with identical dimensions on both legs.

Elipacko manufactures cold-chain-optimized PP corrugated boxes with optional drain hole configurations, reinforced base panels for pallet stacking under high load, and food-grade materials for direct contact applications throughout the cold chain.
