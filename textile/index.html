Why Textile Storage Demands Plastic

Cardboard storage boxes for textiles and garments have one critical flaw: moisture. Cardboard absorbs humidity, transfers acids to fabric over time, and collapses under the weight of stacked textiles in warehouse storage. For industrial fabric operations, archival institutions, or garment distribution, this translates directly into product damage and claims.

PP corrugated textile boxes are acid-free by material composition — polypropylene contains no lignin, sulfur, or acidic compounds that migrate to stored fabric. They're 100% moisture-proof, so humidity cycling in warehouse environments doesn't compromise structural integrity or transfer moisture to stored goods. And they maintain exact stacking dimensions indefinitely, unlike cardboard that compresses and creates unstable stacks over time.
