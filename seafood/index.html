Why Seafood Packers Switch to PP Corrugated

Seafood packaging is one of the most demanding environments for any box material. Constant moisture, ice melt, brine, and temperature cycling destroy standard cardboard within hours. Wax-coated cardboard performs marginally better — but still degrades, can't be sanitized, and ends up in landfill after a single use.

PP corrugated plastic boxes solve every one of these failure modes. The polypropylene hollow-sheet construction is 100% waterproof, impervious to brine and fish oils, pressure-washable, and rated for repeated use across 50+ cycles. For high-volume seafood packers and distributors, the total cost advantage vs. wax cardboard is substantial.

Elipacko manufactures these boxes in food-grade polypropylene with optional drain holes, vented walls for ice drainage, and reinforced stacking capability for loaded pallets in cold storage. 0% import duty to the USA and USA manufacturing coming soon make Elipacko the strongest direct-source option for North American buyers.

Common Questions from Seafood Buyers

Are these boxes safe for direct contact with fresh seafood?

Yes. Elipacko uses food-grade polypropylene that is FDA-compliant and safe for direct contact with fresh and frozen seafood, shellfish, and fish products. The material contains no plasticizers or additives that leach into food.

Can you add drain holes in the base?

Yes — drain holes in the base, walls, or both are available as a standard customization. Hole diameter and pattern are configurable based on your ice drainage requirements.

How do these compare to EPS (Styrofoam) seafood boxes?

PP corrugated boxes are reusable (50+ cycles vs. single-use EPS), fully recyclable (EPS is not widely accepted), and mechanically stronger under full-load stacking. For high-volume operations, the total cost per use is dramatically lower.
