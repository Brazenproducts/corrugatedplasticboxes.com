The Logistics Case for Foldable Boxes

Standard rigid boxes are efficient when full — but every empty return trip wastes space and money. For operations running reusable packaging programs, return logistics are often the biggest cost driver.

Foldable PP corrugated boxes collapse to approximately 25% of their erected volume. A truck that returns 4 loads of rigid empties can now return 1 load of folded empties — cutting return freight costs by up to 75%. For distribution centers and manufacturers running closed-loop packaging systems, this single characteristic often justifies the entire switch to plastic.

Elipacko manufactures foldable PP corrugated boxes in any footprint and depth. Standard fold-flat designs use integrated PP hinge strips at the score lines — no separate hardware. More robust designs add edge profiles and corner fittings for heavy-load applications that still need to fold for return.

How Foldable PP Boxes Work in Practice

1

Erect

Fold up in seconds — PP hinge lines snap into place, tabs or Velcro secure the base

2

Fill & Ship

Load product, close lid or tuck flaps. Stacks safely on pallets for outbound shipment

3

Unload

Recipient empties box, folds flat in seconds — no tools, no disassembly required

4

Return

Stack folded flats — 4x more boxes per truck. Return freight cost drops dramatically
